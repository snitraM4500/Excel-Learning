What should you calculate here?

The bakery prepares fresh items every Monday morning. By the end of the week, some items have been sold and some are left over. If you know how many items were baked at the beginning of the week and how many were sold during the week, how do you find out how many are left?

Hint: Think about what happens when you start with a certain amount and give some away. What math operation helps you find what remains?

‚ùì Question 2: Column G - Profit per Item

What should you calculate here?

Every baked good has a cost to make (ingredients, electricity, etc.) and a selling price. The profit is what the bakery actually earns after covering the cost. How do you calculate the profit for each item sold?

Hint: If making a cupcake costs 18 MZN and you sell it for 45 MZN, what calculation shows you the actual profit from that sale?

‚ùì Question 3: Column H - Weekly Sales

What should you calculate here?

Weekly Sales represents the total money collected from selling these items. If you sold a certain number of items and each item has a specific price, how do you calculate the total money received?

Hint: Imagine you sold 72 cupcakes at 45 MZN each. Which mathematical operation would help you find the total money earned from cupcake sales?

‚ùì Question 4: Column I - Weekly Profit

What should you calculate here?

The Weekly Profit is different from Weekly Sales because it accounts for the cost of making the items. You already calculated the profit per item (Column G). Now you need to find the total profit from all items sold this week.

Hint: You know the profit made on each individual item and you know how many items were sold. How do these two numbers combine to give you total profit?

Hint: You need to add up all the numbers in specific columns. There's a function designed exactly for this purpose - it adds a range of cells together. Which columns contain numbers that make sense to add up?
